Recent Work

Case Study

Data-Driven Error Reduction

The Challenge A data import process was generating hundreds of errors daily. The team was spending significant time handling these case-by-case, treating each error as a unique problem to solve.
The Approach Rather than treating symptoms, I analyzed the error patterns using database queries. By joining data from multiple sources and grouping errors by frequency, I identified the true distribution of problems—something that wasn't visible when handling errors one at a time.

The Result: 75% of all errors stemmed from just 1-2 root causes. Two targeted fixes eliminated the majority of the problem—work that would have taken weeks of case-by-case handling.

The fix wasn't more error handling code. It was asking the right questions of the data.

Case Study

PTA Signup Friction Analysis

The Challenge Parents signing up for their school's PTA through a national membership site face a frustrating experience—scrolling through long dropdown lists to find their specific PTA among thousands of options.
The Analysis This UX friction likely causes abandonment and reduces PTA membership. The current interface puts the burden on users to locate their organization, when the PTA already knows exactly which organization they want parents to join.
The Proposed Solution Allow PTAs to generate direct signup links with URL parameters that pre-select their organization. PTAs could share these links in school communications, eliminating the search friction entirely.

Expected Impact: Higher conversion rates, less frustration, more PTA members—achieved by removing a step rather than adding features.

Sometimes the biggest impact comes from removing friction, not adding features.
